Choices Give us a Voice

Alternative and augmentative communication gives voice to thousands of people who might not otherwise be heard. Supporting these communicators is a community effort, and requires collaboration between practitioners, family members, developers and, well, all of us!.

AAC advancements, planning ahead for growth, strides in technology, expanding understanding in the community & more -- we are THRILLED for the chance to share AAC insights from experienced voices this summer.

We invite you to take part in this AAC focused, online conference with sessions addressing a large variety of topics. The event will also include fun activities, inspirational stories, and even some fun surprises!

For Communicators

The first priority with a communication system is the individual. CoughDrop has vocabulary sets for many different levels, with built-in supports and vocabulary progression tools to start basic and grow over time.

CoughDrop is one of the most flexible apps available, with support for eye tracking and switches, and also new input methods like joysticks and gesture control, to give the user more flexibility.

See how Families Use CoughDrop

For Families

Learning to communicate takes help from the people around us. CoughDrop families can use the app on all their devices, and it includes activity ideas and strategies to help families and friends learn how they can help make communication happen.

See how Teams Use CoughDrop

For Practitioners and Staff

Experts and staff need to be able to see what's working and what isn't. CoughDrop has built-in reports and goal-setting tools to keep everyone on the same page and track progress over time.
